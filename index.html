Core Environmental Framework

US environmental policy is governed by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), created in 1970 to consolidate federal research and monitoring.

🌬️

Clean Air Act

Regulates hazardous air pollutants. For agriculture, this involves managing ammonia from livestock and dust from tilling.

💧

Clean Water Act

Focuses on "Point Source" and "Non-Point Source" pollution, critical for managing fertilizer runoff into streams.

🦌

ESA (1973)

The Endangered Species Act protects critical habitats, often limiting where and how farmers can use certain pesticides.

☣️

FIFRA

Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act. Governs the registration, distribution, and use of all pesticides in the US.

Spotlight on Idaho

The Importance of Public Lands

Idaho is unique because over 60% of its land is managed by the federal government (BLM and Forest Service). This creates a complex legal environment where agricultural grazing rights on public land are a major topic of legislation.

01

Water Rights: Idaho uses "Prior Appropriation" (First in time, first in right), unlike the riparian systems common in Europe.

02

Dairy Leadership: As the 3rd largest dairy producer in the US, Idaho has specific state-level laws for manure management.

US Farm Bill vs. EU Common Agricultural Policy

Environmental Compliance

In Italy, EU "Cross-Compliance" makes environmental protection mandatory to receive subsidies. In the US, many programs are voluntary and incentive-based through the NRCS (Natural Resources Conservation Service).

Pesticide Regulation

The EU (and Italy) often uses the "Precautionary Principle," leading to faster bans on certain chemicals compared to the US "Risk-Benefit" analysis approach.
