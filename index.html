The Federal Framework

In the United States, environmental protection is a shared responsibility between the **Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)** and individual states. For students in the agricultural sector, these laws define the boundaries of pesticide use, water irrigation, and livestock management.

Spotlight: Idaho

The Gem State Perspective

Our student teacher comes from Idaho, a state where **63% of the land is managed by the federal government**. This creates a unique legal landscape for farmers, who must often navigate federal grazing permits and strict water rights under the "Prior Appropriation" doctrine.

"In Idaho, water is more than a resource; it is a legal property right that can be bought and sold independently of the land."

USA vs Italy: A Comparative Study

🇺🇸 US Model

  • ● Focus on "Risk-Benefit" analysis for pesticides.
  • ● Voluntary conservation incentives via the "Farm Bill".
  • ● Private property rights are heavily protected.

🇮🇹 Italian/EU Model

  • ● Based on the "Precautionary Principle".
  • ● Mandatory "Cross-Compliance" for EU subsidies.
  • ● Focus on "DOP/IGP" and integrated pest management.
