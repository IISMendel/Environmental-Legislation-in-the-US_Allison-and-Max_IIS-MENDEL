Legislative Foundations

In the United States, environmental protection is governed by a robust framework of **Federal Acts**. For our students in Villa Cortese, it is essential to understand that while Italy follows EU directives, the US relies on the **EPA (Environmental Protection Agency)** to enforce national standards that directly impact farm operations, from pesticide drift to manure management.

Spotlight: Idaho Agriculture

Our student teacher joins us from **Idaho**, a state where nature and industry are deeply intertwined. Unlike Italy, Idaho's environmental challenges are shaped by massive federal land holdings (63%) and a strict "First in time, first in right" water doctrine.

Water Property Rights

In Idaho, water is a private property right that can be traded, which is essential for the irrigation of the famous Idaho potatoes.

Public Land Management

Ranchers must navigate complex Federal Bureau of Land Management (BLM) rules to graze cattle on public territories.

USA (Statutes) vs. Italy (Directives)

US Agricultural Policy

  • ★ **Risk-Benefit Analysis**: Economic gain is weighed against environmental risk.
  • ★ **Voluntary Programs**: The Farm Bill pays farmers for "Green" actions (EQIP).
  • ★ **Statutory Focus**: Laws target specific points (Clean Water Act, FIFRA).

Italian/EU Policy

  • ● **Precautionary Principle**: Substances are banned if a risk is suspected.
  • ● **Cross-Compliance**: PAC funds are only paid if ecological rules are met.
  • ● **Integrated Directives**: Broad frameworks like the Nitrate Directive.
